Educational Technology Course, January 2019 - May 2019

üëÄ So, what's the sitch?

  • High school drumline students work hard in the fall to learn playing technique, how to prepare for a half-time show, and other music.
  • While spring training practices are in place, not everyone is able to attend consistently due to varying commitments.
  • Implementing an alternative form of teaching and learning would give more options for students to keep their playing in shape.

ü§î Exploring the problem space

  • I helped create a survey to understand the students' interest levels with traditional music methods and instruction.
  • My teammate gave music lessons at his old high school, so he distributed the survey to marching band students there.

üîç Initial findings

  • Many students preferred performing when given the options of performing, composing, or both.
  • They had several reasons for learning music: it was fun and provided outlets for self-expression, creativity, collaboration, and feelings of accomplishment.
  • The majority of students learn music outside of school through listening, lessons, YouTube videos, books, and gigs.
  • Those who did not learn music outside of school either wanted to learn or didn‚Äôt have enough time.
  • Guitar tabs, sheet music, and teachers have been beneficial resources.
  • Students disliked ‚Äúrepeating songs over and over again‚Äù and not being able to choose what they played.
  • They complained about the varying levels of players; when their peers didn‚Äôt practice, they felt held back.

üí° Refining our learning goals

  • To introduce informal methods of music education to marching band drummers, preparing them for the upcoming drumline season.
  • Allows marching band drummers to practice outside of school and relate it to what is being rehearsed in school spring rehearsals.
  • Provides marching band drummers with the required skills needed to succeed in the season and have a head start.

üß† Early prototyping

  • During our brainstorming sessions, we prioritized 5 key concepts: Interest-based learning, Informal learning, Connected learning, Constructionist approach to learning, and Personal connections.
  • We wanted to focus on the social aspect because we believed that it would resonate with students.
  • I drew some sketches of our ideas on paper and later migrated it to Adobe XD.

üë• Thinking out loud

  • One of my teammates interviewed roughly 25 drumline students, split in groups of approximately 3 or 4.
  • Within the small groups, each individual was asked to perform one of four specific tasks: (1) log in, (2) find an instructor video, (3) find a video based on personal preference, and (4) post a video.

üëç Yays

  • Students said that our app was good for not having time to go see instructors.
  • Our tabs for interests and instructor recommended content were also well received. Students liked that our app was aimed at helping them find they want, whether it was a certain category or based on their skillset.
  • Students believed that our app was easy to navigate.
  • They also liked the simple color scheme.

üëé Nays

  • It wasn't clear what tab the user was on.
  • The process of posting new content wasn‚Äôt very intuitive. We later streamlined the login process and provided clear options.
  • We also learned that one of the students was actually red-green color blind. Because it is important for our prototype to be accessible, we made sure to change our color scheme. The color blind student recommended a light blue.
