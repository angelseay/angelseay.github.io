Augmented Reality Research Intern

Georgia Tech Research Institute, May 2018 - July 2018

Over the summer, I worked with two other students and created a multi-platform augmented reality client with Apple’s ARKit and Google’s ARCore. I had no prior experience with Unity, C#, or any AR tools.

I was mainly responsible for the map-related UI elements. I used the MapBox SDK to display a 3D map where spatial meshes floated over their approximate GPS locations. I also created a minimap feature since the SDK didn't include one. The locations of different devices using our app could be shared and displayed on this minimap.

In addition to the MapBox features, I migrated our ARKit app (iOS) to ARCore (Android). It was overall a fairly smooth transition, except I needed to accept certain SSL certificates (otherwise it would crash) and also use ARCore’s form of image detection since ARKit's wasn't compatible.

I also added toggles for these features in a menu to make sure there weren't any hidden modes for users.
